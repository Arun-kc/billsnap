# BillSnap Product Brief

## What BillSnap is
BillSnap is a lightweight bill and receipt digitization tool for Indian small businesses. A shop owner can snap a photo of a bill, review the extracted information, and export clean monthly data for an accountant.

## Who it is for
Primary user:
- Non-tech-savvy small shop owner
- Usually 40s to 60s
- Smartphone-first
- Comfortable with WhatsApp, not spreadsheets
- Based in South India, starting with Kerala

First user:
- Arun's father
- Electric shop owner
- Currently relies on Arun to manually enter bill data into Excel every quarter

## Main problem
Bills pile up over weeks or months. At quarter end, someone has to manually type details from each bill into Excel. This is slow, tiring, error-prone, and creates dependence on a family member.

## Core value
BillSnap removes the manual typing burden.

The promise is:
- No more typing bills by hand
- Ready for your accountant in minutes, not hours
- Works in your language, at your pace

## V1 success signal
The user should feel: “That was easy and it saved a lot of time.”

## V1 product scope
Included:
- Upload bill image or PDF
- OCR extraction
- Review and correction screen
- Monthly export to CSV/Excel
- Single-tenant use
- Mobile-first web experience

Not included:
- Full bookkeeping
- GST filing
- Multi-org support
- Email workflows
- Heavy automation beyond OCR plus user review

## Experience principles
- Friendly, patient, reassuring tone
- Very low cognitive load
- Large buttons and simple forms
- Guide the user one step at a time
- Assume low trust in automation until user confirms extracted data

## Brand direction
Name: BillSnap
Tagline: Snap a bill. Done.

Brand traits:
- Effortless
- Trustworthy
- Local

Tone references:
- Swiggy friendliness
- Apple clarity
- Airbnb trust

## Language guidance
- English UI by default
- Malayalam helper labels for key actions in V1
- Full language toggle can wait until later versions

## Cost and pace constraints
- OCR plus hosting budget should stay roughly within ₹1,000 to ₹5,000 per month initially
- Build pace is around 8 hours per week
- Prefer simple solutions that can be maintained cheaply

## Phase plan
Phase 1:
- Build a version that works well for Arun's father without external help

Phase 2:
- Onboard 3 to 5 friendly local businesses
- Learn from real usage and review time
- Improve based on mistakes, confusion, and export quality
